Grief doesn't lift. It teaches you how to walk with weight. A slow conversation with myself about loss.

## What grief is not

Grief is not a problem to solve. Not a phase to push through. Not a feeling to schedule into a Tuesday afternoon.

Grief is a presence. It moves in. It rearranges the furniture.

## What grief actually does

What grief does, slowly, is teach you a new gravity.

The first weeks, you forget the weight is there and try to move the way you used to. You fall down. A lot.

Then, gradually, your body learns. You walk slower. You talk slower. You stop expecting yourself to be the person you were before.

That's not collapse. That's adaptation.

## The mistake

The mistake is trying to "get over" grief. To return to the old gravity. To act like nothing changed.

You changed. The weight is permanent. But weight, lived with, becomes strength.

## A small practice

Once a week, sit somewhere quiet and say out loud — _I miss you._ Just that. No agenda, no fixing.

That's the whole practice. Repeat for the rest of your life.
