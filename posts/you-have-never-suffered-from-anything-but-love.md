There is a line from the Argentine psychoanalyst Gabriel Rolón that has been sitting in my chest like a small stone:

> "In thirty years, I have hardly ever seen anyone suffer from anything other than love."

Read it again. Slowly.

Not money. Not failure. Not health. Not even death, death itself doesn't hurt, the people left behind do. Every funeral you have ever cried at was a love bill coming due. Every breakup, every estrangement, every quiet midnight where you stared at the ceiling and could not name what was wrong, love. Always love.

You suffer for the partner who left. For the parent who died. For the child who grew up and stopped needing you. For the body you used to have. For the version of yourself who still believed in things. For the youth that walked out one morning without saying goodbye. None of those are different sufferings. They are the same suffering, wearing different masks. Love withdrawn. Love unreturned. Love expired.

And if that's true, and I think it is, then the most important question of your life is not who you love. It is how much love you have left for the one person you can never leave.

Yourself.
