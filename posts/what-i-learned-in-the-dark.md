The year I stopped pretending was the year I started becoming. Notes from the bottom.

## The wall

I had everything I'd asked for — and underneath it I was suffocating. The career was working. The plan was on track. Nothing was wrong, technically. But every morning I woke up tired in a way that sleep didn't fix.

That tired had a name. I just couldn't say it out loud yet.

## What the dark teaches

What I learned in the dark is that most of our suffering is not the dark itself — it's the pretending. The performance of being fine. The energy it takes to keep the surface still.

When you stop performing, the dark becomes quiet. And quiet, it turns out, is where things grow.

## The other side

I'm not on "the other side" of anything. I just stopped pretending the dark wasn't there. And that single shift changed everything else.

This is the work I do with people now. Not because I have answers, but because I know what the dark sounds like.
