If you keep starting over, you're not lazy. You're listening to something. Here's how to hear it.

## Stuckness is not failure

When you keep starting over — new plan, new routine, new resolution, all crashing within a week — the easy story is _I lack discipline._

I don't think that's true.

I think your stuckness is _trying to tell you something._ The repeated failure is the message. You just haven't decoded it yet.

## What stuckness usually means

In ten years of doing this work, the message under chronic stuckness has almost always been one of three things:

1. **You're chasing the wrong thing.** The goal you keep failing at isn't actually yours.
2. **You're skipping a step.** There's a foundational piece missing that no amount of discipline can substitute for.
3. **You're afraid of what success would mean.** Part of you doesn't want to arrive.

## How to decode yours

Get specific. Write down the last three things you tried to start and quit. Look at them side by side.

Ask: _what are these three failures collectively telling me?_

The answer is usually obvious — and usually uncomfortable. That's how you know you found it.
