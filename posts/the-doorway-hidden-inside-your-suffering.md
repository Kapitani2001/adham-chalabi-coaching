Most people treat their suffering as a problem to be eliminated. What if it's actually a door — and you've been pushing on the wrong side?

## The mistake we all make

When something hurts, the first instinct is to make it stop. Numb it, distract from it, push past it. We treat pain like a malfunction.

But pain isn't a malfunction. **It's a signal.** And signals point somewhere.

## What pain is pointing at

The work I do with clients almost always starts with the same move: instead of trying to fix the pain, we get curious about it. We sit with it. We ask it questions.

- What is this pain trying to tell me?
- What part of me does it belong to?
- What would change if I stopped fighting it?

## The doorway

When you stop fighting the pain, something quiet happens. The pain becomes a teacher. It points at the part of your life that's asking to be different.

That's the doorway. It was never a wall.
