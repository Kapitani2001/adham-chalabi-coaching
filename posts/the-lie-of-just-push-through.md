Sometimes the wall is information, not an obstacle. Here's how to tell which one you're actually facing.

## The advice that fails most people

"Just push through it." It's the standard reply when someone admits they're stuck. And sometimes — sometimes — it's the right answer.

But more often, the wall is trying to tell you something. And pushing through it just hurts your hands.

## Two kinds of walls

**Walls of resistance** are the kind you push through. The discomfort of starting. The boredom of the middle. These walls give way when you keep walking.

**Walls of misalignment** are different. These don't move when you push. These are walls that say, _you're walking in the wrong direction._

## How to tell them apart

Ask yourself: do I feel _depleted_ by this work, or do I feel _disconnected_ from it?

- Depleted = wall of resistance. Rest, then push.
- Disconnected = wall of misalignment. Stop, and listen.

The advice that fails most people is treating every wall the same.
