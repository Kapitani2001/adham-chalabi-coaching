Motivation runs out by Wednesday. Meaning shows up uninvited at 3am. Here's why one is fuel and the other is gravity.

## Motivation is a feeling

Motivation is the high you get when you watch a great YouTube video. It feels like power. It lasts about three days.

It's a feeling. And feelings, by design, do not last.

## Meaning is a structure

Meaning is different. Meaning is the answer to the question, _why does this matter to me?_

When you know why something matters, you don't need motivation. You don't need a streak. You don't need a coach hyping you up. You just keep walking — even when it's slow, even when it's quiet, even when nobody's watching.

## How to find your meaning

This isn't something you discover in one afternoon. It's a slow excavation.

Start here: write down three moments in your life where you felt _alive_ — not productive, not impressive, alive. Look for what they share.

That's where your meaning lives.
