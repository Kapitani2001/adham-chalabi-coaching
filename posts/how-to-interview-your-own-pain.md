The most useful question I know — and the silence that has to follow it.

## The question

Here it is. The whole technique:

> _"What are you trying to tell me?"_

You ask this _of the pain itself_. Out loud, ideally. To the tightness in your chest, the knot in your stomach, the heaviness behind your eyes.

It sounds strange. It works.

## Why it works

Most people relate to their pain as an enemy — something to defeat, suppress, or escape. The question reframes it. It treats pain as a messenger.

And messengers, when you finally listen, get quiet.

## The silence that has to follow

Here's the part most people skip. After you ask the question, **you have to wait.**

Not seconds. Sometimes minutes. Sometimes a full afternoon.

The first thing your brain offers up is usually a story you've told yourself a hundred times. Let it pass. Wait for the second thing. The third.

Eventually, something quieter speaks. That's the answer.
